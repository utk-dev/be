Story

"The people who are crazy enough to think they can change the world are the ones who do."
- Rob Siltanen

The story is of a rebellion against the current status quo, to create the space for the young passion to BE what they want to be. A few years back, inequity during Bharat's teenage years around lack of opportunities, guidance, resources and even openness to think differently around career just because he was from a small town & humble background left him extremely disturbed & disheartened.

Whereas 300 km away, Manthan was fascinated & inspired by the true story of how one person changed the life course of his family for the better through entrepreneurship. It was the story of his father. Manthan father's story of entrepreneurial struggle in addition to entrepreneurial experiments ignited the spirit of change. He realised the power of stories & entrepreneurship to bring the multi-generational change. His first raw experience as a micro-entrepreneur, at the age of 15, allowed him to understand & empathise with the struggle of becoming self-reliant and getting a dignified livelihood.

When they met, both were restless due to injustice & inequity in access to opportunities & future lives of youth getting decided based on their birthplace & economic conditions. With love, hope & trust, they embarked on the journey of Bharat Manthan to challenge the status quo and work tirelessly for a future, where youth will be able to follow their true passion & purpose and BE what they want to be. They see entrepreneurship as a key to self-expression, creativity and to this vision.

In 2019, they laid the foundation of BE in the hope it would become a decentralised institution to nurture entrepreneurial leaders who will serve to create a more equitable & sustainable world through the harmony of head, heart & hand.

Problem Statement: Our monocultural education system has created a mass scarcity mindset with a narrow definition of success focused on the fearful pursuit of material excess leading to disconnect with the inner self, lack of meaningful opportunities & economic and ecological crisis. We want to challenge this multilayered triangle of hierarchy.
